Frequently asked questions

Start with the right questions.

What is a Preliminary Site Assessment?

It is an early-stage review that helps you understand possible development pathways, important site considerations and the most practical next step for your property.

Should I order a Site Assessment before buying a property?

A Site Assessment may help you better understand a property before committing to a purchase, but it should not replace independent legal, financial, planning or building advice.

Can you tell me exactly how many dwellings I can build?

Early assessments can indicate possible options, but final outcomes depend on detailed investigations, planning controls, design, authority requirements and formal approvals.

What is the difference between a Site Assessment and a Feasibility Report?

A Site Assessment is an early-stage review of the property and possible development pathways. A Feasibility Report goes further by examining the financial viability, costs, risks and likely returns of a proposed development.

Can iSubdivide help with both permits and construction?

Yes. iSubdivide can support projects from early assessment and planning through design, approvals, construction coordination and delivery, depending on the property and project scope.

What happens after I receive my assessment?

The team can explain the findings and recommend the next appropriate step, which may include further feasibility, planning, design or a broader development discussion.
